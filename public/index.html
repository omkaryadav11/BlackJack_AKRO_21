Objective of the Game

The goal in blackjack is to have a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer’s hand without exceeding 21. If you exceed 21, you "bust" and lose the hand.

Card Values

  • Number Cards (2-10): Face value (e.g., a 5 is worth 5 points).
  • Face Cards (Jack, Queen, King): Each worth 10 points.
  • Aces: Worth 1 point or 11 points, depending on which value benefits the hand more.

Game Setup

  • Players: Up to 4 players can participate.
  • Dealer: A designated dealer manages the game, dealing cards and handling bets.
  • Deck: The game typically uses one to eight standard decks of 52 cards.
  • Table Layout: The table has designated betting areas for each player and one for the dealer.

Starting the Game

  • Placing Bets: Each player places their bet in their designated area before any cards are dealt. Minimum and maximum bet limits should be established.
  • Dealing Cards:
    • Each player is dealt two cards face up.
    • The dealer is dealt two cards: one face up (the "upcard") and one face down (the "hole card").

Player Actions

After the initial deal, players take turns in a clockwise direction. Each player can choose from the following actions:

  • Hit: Request an additional card. Players can hit as many times as they like until they either stand or bust.
  • Stand: Keep the current hand and end their turn.
  • Double Down: Double the initial bet and receive exactly one additional card (only after the first two cards).
  • Split: If the two cards are of the same value, players can split them into two separate hands.
  • Surrender: Forfeit half the bet and end the hand early (if allowed).

Dealer's Turn

After all players have completed their actions, the dealer reveals their hole card. The dealer must follow these rules:

  • Stand on 17 or higher: The dealer must stand on a "hard" 17 and may also stand on a "soft" 17.
  • Hit on 16 or lower: The dealer must hit until reaching at least 17.

Winning the Game

The outcomes are determined as follows:

  • If a player has a hand value higher than the dealer’s without busting, the player wins and receives a payout of 1:1 on their bet.
  • If a player busts, they lose their bet.
  • If the dealer busts, all remaining players win and receive a payout of 1:1.
  • If the player and dealer have the same hand value (push), the player retains their bet.
